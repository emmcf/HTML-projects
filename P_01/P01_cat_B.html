Rocks glass

A short, sturdy glass with a thick base. It is mainly used to serve spirits neat or on the rocks, such as whiskey, and cocktails such as the Old Fashioned.

Martini glass

A glass with a distinctive "V" shape, a long stem and a circular base. It is designed to keep the drink cold without being warmed by the heat of your hand. It is used to serve martinis and other straight-up cocktails.

Boston glass

Conical glass that forms part of a "Boston shaker" when used in conjunction with a Boston tin. It is made of durable glass to withstand intensive use and can also be used as a mixing glass.

Cognac glass

A glass with a wide base and a narrow opening. This shape helps to concentrate the aromas of the cognac and allows the drink to be warmed by hand to release its flavours and aromas.

Long drink glass

Tall, slim glass that holds 240-350ml. Used to serve cocktails with ice and a large proportion of non-alcoholic mixer, such as the classic Highball.
